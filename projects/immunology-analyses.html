CIC-ATXN1L Complex in IFN and ISG Regulation

This analysis explores the role of the CIC-ATXN1L complex as a transcriptional repressor in the regulation of interferon (IFN) and interferon-stimulated genes (ISGs). Under homeostatic conditions, CIC-ATXN1L binds to an 8-nucleotide motif near IFN and ISG promoters, preventing their aberrant activation. During respiratory viral infections, this repression is lifted due to the degradation of CIC-ATXN1L, mediated by activation of the Mitogen-activated protein kinase (MAPK) pathway. This mechanism primes host cells for robust antiviral responses through interferon regulatory factors (IRFs) and signal transducer and activator of transcription (STAT) transcription factors.

Data Description

The dataset includes gene expression profiles under homeostasis and viral infection conditions:

  • RNA sequencing (RNA-seq) data capturing transcriptional changes following CIC-ATXN1L depletion
  • Experimental validation including knockout (KO) and overexpression models in human and murine cell lines

Data Source

GEO accession GSE271898

Key Findings

  • CIC-ATXN1L as a repressor: Binds directly to IFN and ISG promoters, suppressing their activation in the absence of infection.
  • MAPK pathway activation: Viral infection induces MAPK signaling, leading to CIC-ATXN1L degradation and relief of repression.
  • Conserved mechanism: Similar regulatory patterns in murine models suggest evolutionary conservation.
  • Functional consequences: Loss of CIC-ATXN1L results in spontaneous IFN and ISG expression, potentially leading to auto-inflammatory responses.

Methods

  • RNA-seq analysis: Differential gene expression between control and CIC-ATXN1L-deficient conditions.
  • Functional assays: Antiviral response strength and impacted pathways following CIC-ATXN1L depletion.

Antigen Processing & Presentation Pathway — Cross-Tissue Tumor Differential Gene Expression

This project uses bioinformatic analysis of gene expression data from multiple solid tumor tissues to explore whether observed heterogeneity arises from tumor-intrinsic mutations or from immune modulation within the tumor microenvironment (TME). The work centers on the antigen processing and presentation pathway and its differential expression across normal and tumor samples.

It includes mass data cleaning and mining pipelines (e.g. Capstone_Data_Cleaning.ipynb, Capstone_Data_Mining.ipynb) and uses Normal vs. Tumor tissue comparisons to characterize pathway-level and gene-level differences.

Early Dendritic Cell Maturation

This project studies early-stage dendritic cell maturation using transcriptomic data. The dataset consists of RNA sequencing data from early-stage dendritic cells at different maturation states. The primary goal is to understand transcriptional changes during maturation and identify key regulatory pathways.

Data Source

GEO accession GSE288378

Methods

  • Preprocess raw RNA-seq count data into log-transformed expression values.
  • Run individual scripts for boxplot, heatmap, PCA, and GSEA.
  • Interpret visual outputs to identify key genes and pathways involved in dendritic cell maturation.

Results & Interpretation

  • Boxplots highlight differentially expressed genes.
  • Heatmaps cluster genes and samples based on expression similarity.
  • PCA plots reveal major sources of variance in gene expression.
  • GSEA identifies enriched pathways regulating early dendritic cell maturation.
